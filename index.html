Industrial IoT Software: An Independent Guide

Industrial IoT software evaluation splits along two different problem lines. If your challenge is connecting a platform to an existing OT environment — PLCs, historians, SCADA systems, legacy assets running proprietary protocols — the variables that determine fit are integration depth, hardware affinity, and deployment architecture. If your challenge is managing thousands or millions of distributed devices across dispersed infrastructure, the criteria shift to device provisioning, fleet management, connectivity reliability, and visibility at scale. Most buying processes treat these as the same decision. They are not.

What this site covers

Industrial IoT software spans connectivity middleware, edge data platforms, cloud infrastructure, analytics and historian systems, and full-stack automation suites. The vendor landscape cuts across automation giants with deep hardware affinity, challengers built for specific verticals, and cloud providers whose platforms require significant engineering to make useful in an industrial context.

Every section is organized around a buyer decision. The landscape maps the market. The tools support active evaluation. The comparisons isolate trade-offs between platforms with overlapping positioning. The guides go deeper on specific topics without the vendor angle.

Why independent

Most IIoT platform content is produced by vendors, funded by system integrators with preferred partnerships, or written by analysts whose scoring methodology is not visible. This site has no vendor relationships, no sponsored content, and no affiliate arrangements with the platforms it covers.

When this site has an opinion, it says so. When the evidence is thin, it says that too.
