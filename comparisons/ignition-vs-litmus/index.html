Ignition vs. Litmus

Ignition and Litmus both support MQTT, Sparkplug B, and UNS architectures — but they arrive at connectivity from opposite directions. Ignition is a full industrial application platform where connectivity is one layer of a broader SCADA, HMI, and MES stack. Litmus is purpose-built for the connectivity and edge compute problem, without the application platform overhead. The comparison comes down to whether you need the full Ignition platform or a focused edge-and-connectivity layer.

Ignition wins when
  • You need SCADA, HMI, or MES capabilities alongside connectivity — Ignition handles all of them
  • You are building a UNS architecture and want the most mature Sparkplug B ecosystem via Cirrus Link
  • The flat-rate licensing model delivers better economics than per-node pricing at your scale
  • You need reporting, OEE dashboards, or production tracking built into the same platform
  • You want access to the large Ignition integrator network and Exchange marketplace
Litmus wins when
  • You need to run containerized applications — ML models, quality logic, local analytics — at the edge alongside connectivity
  • Your facilities have unreliable WAN and you need fully autonomous offline edge operation
  • You are managing connectivity across many distributed sites and need centralized node fleet management
  • You have deep legacy protocol requirements and want native driver coverage without upstream translation layers
  • You want a focused tool without the overhead of a full application platform
The real decision

If you are modernizing a SCADA or MES environment and want connectivity bundled into the same platform that handles visualization and reporting, Ignition is the stronger fit. If your architecture is cloud-native and analytics already live upstream, and your problem is specifically getting clean normalized data from the edge to the cloud — with the ability to run compute at the edge — Litmus is the more focused solution.

These platforms are often complementary rather than competitive. Litmus handles edge-to-MQTT; Ignition handles MQTT-to-application. Evaluate whether your architecture needs both layers or whether one platform can handle the scope you actually need before treating them as an either/or decision.
