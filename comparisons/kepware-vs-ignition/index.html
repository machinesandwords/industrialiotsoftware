Kepware vs. Ignition

Kepware and Ignition are the two most commonly co-evaluated platforms by controls engineers, but they are solving different problems. Kepware is a dedicated protocol translation and OPC server. Ignition is a full industrial application platform that handles SCADA, HMI, MES, and IIoT — with connectivity as one of its layers. Choosing between them depends on whether you need a focused connectivity tool or a platform that can anchor a broader operational architecture.

Kepware wins when
  • Your environment has significant legacy hardware — DH+, DH-485, older Siemens — where driver field history is the deciding factor
  • Your architecture is OPC-DA or OPC-UA based and you need the broadest client compatibility
  • You need a focused connectivity tool without platform overhead
  • Your existing SCADA and HMI are already handled by another platform
  • You want to minimize the footprint of the connectivity layer
Ignition wins when
  • You are building or replacing a SCADA, HMI, or MES system and want connectivity bundled
  • You are building a UNS architecture with Sparkplug B and want native MQTT Transmission support
  • The flat-rate unlimited licensing model delivers better economics at scale than per-driver pricing
  • You need reporting, OEE calculation, or production tracking alongside connectivity
  • You are modernizing multiple systems simultaneously and want a single platform vendor
The real decision

These two platforms are often used together — Kepware handling legacy protocol translation and feeding Ignition as an OPC-UA client. If you are evaluating them as alternatives rather than complements, the question is scope. Kepware is the right answer if connectivity is a bounded, well-defined problem. Ignition is the right answer if you are building an operational architecture and connectivity is one piece of a larger modernization.

Licensing economics often drive the decision at scale. Kepware's per-driver model can compound significantly across a large tag count. Ignition's flat-rate model becomes increasingly attractive as the deployment grows. Run the TCO comparison against your actual tag count and site footprint before shortlisting either.
